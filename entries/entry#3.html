Journal Entry #3

To answer this question I have to give some background on me and my development. I’ve always been a slow developer socially, it wasn’t even until 2nd semester sophomore year of high school that I finally started developing SOME leadership skills. Thus, I didn’t fully understand how society worked either. So, I view my greatest mistake so far as when I was around 9 or 10 and stole my Grandma’s credit card to buy things online. I didn’t even do it maliciously, I had just seen on the screen that it was asking for a card number and I remembered seeing numbers on my Grandma’s credit card. I had no concept of money then, so I had thought it would appear out of nowhere. I spent over $300 just on different games online on my computer; surprisingly, I still play one of those games to this day. I was in so much trouble, it was horrible; but, I learned what money was that day. And I have never stolen money since then. So I would say it was an important part of my development, I just wish I had learned it without spending $300. And by the way, my Grandma never got that money back from the companies. I was grounded for life from those games and was monitored very closely afterward. But I think having access to a computer at such a young age was what sprouted my love for computer science, so I would say it wasn’t all bad. However, I still feel bad about what I did to this day, especially since it was from my Grandma, the person who raised me. But without this happening, who knows how long after it would’ve been before I discovered the value of money. And not just the value, but the fact that money isn’t just a concept that’s thrown around.
